Karan Mukhi
I am a final year DPhil (PhD) student in Computer Science at the University of Oxford, in the Oxford Control and Verification group under the supervision of Alessandro Abate. Previously, I earned an MPhys in Theoretical Physics from the University of Manchester.

My research focuses on robust, computationally tractable optimisation methods for large-scale power systems. I focus on coordinating populations of controllable domestic devices, such as EVs and heat pumps, and creating practical tools that enable aggregators to unlock their flexibility. I care a lot about building a sustainable energy system.

Prior to my PhD, I worked at National Grid ET as a data scientist, and at Electron as a software engineer. I also collaborated with Open Climate Fix on the development of a mobile app, and I interned at CCFE working on nuclear fusion research.
